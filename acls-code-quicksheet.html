0Adult cardiac arrest algorithm — flowchart

1Adult Cardiac Arrest — universal flow

  1. Verify unresponsive · activate code · start high-quality CPR.
  2. Attach pads / monitor ASAP — read rhythm.
  3. Branch to Shockable (VF / pVT) or Non-shockable (asystole / PEA).
  4. Pulse + rhythm checks every 2 min; switch compressor each cycle.
  5. Treat H's & T's in parallel.
  6. If ROSC → post-arrest care (§7).

High-quality CPR: rate 100–120/min; depth 2–2.4 in (5–6 cm) — avoid > 6 cm (2025); full chest recoil; minimize interruptions (compression fraction > 60%); firm surface; 30:2 pre-airway; 1 breath / 6 sec (10/min) post-airway. Avoid hyperventilation. Continuous waveform EtCO₂: confirms tube, gauges CPR quality, signals ROSC.

Unified 6-link Chain of Survival (2025): recognition → activation → high-quality CPR → early defibrillation → post-arrest care → recovery.

2VF / pulseless VT (Shockable)

  1. SHOCK 120–200 J biphasic (or 360 J mono); resume CPR immediately × 2 min.
  2. Rhythm check → if persistent VF/pVT, SHOCK again. Establish IV/IO.
  3. Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO q3–5 min (after 2nd shock).
  4. Resume CPR × 2 min → rhythm check → SHOCK if persistent.
  5. Amiodarone 300 mg IV bolus (after 3rd shock); repeat 150 mg once. Or lidocaine 1–1.5 mg/kg, then 0.5–0.75 mg/kg.
  6. Treat reversible causes (§4). Consider advanced airway (ETT or supraglottic).
  7. Refractory VF: vector change & double-sequential defibrillation usefulness NOT established (2025; recurrent VF after a successful shock is more common than true shock failure); consider IV β-blocker (esmolol), Mg if torsades, eCPR if program available.

3Asystole / PEA (Non-shockable)

  1. CPR continues; do NOT shock.
  2. Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO ASAP, then q3–5 min.
  3. Aggressive H's & T's hunt — PEA is a cause-driven rhythm.
  4. Confirm asystole in ≥ 2 leads / increase gain — fine VF can mimic.
  5. Consider advanced airway; capnography (EtCO₂ < 10 mmHg with high-quality CPR for 20 min and no reversible cause = poor prognosis → discuss termination).
  6. Bedside echo (PoCUS) at pulse check (< 10 sec): tamponade, RV strain, cardiac standstill.

5Bradycardia (HR < 50, symptomatic)

  1. Assess perfusion: hypotension, AMS, ischemic pain, acute HF, shock?
  2. Atropine 1 mg IV q3–5 min, max 3 mg (2020 update; previously 0.5 mg).
  3. If atropine fails or unstable → transcutaneous pacing + analgesia/sedation.
  4. Or chronotropic infusion: dopamine 5–20 mcg/kg/min or epinephrine 2–10 mcg/min.
  5. Treat cause (ischemia, drug effect, ↑K, hypothyroid). Transvenous pacing if persistent.
  6. Avoid atropine in 2°-Mobitz II / 3° block with wide QRS — escape may worsen; pace.

6Tachycardia with pulse (HR > 150 typical)

Unstable (hypotension, AMS, ischemia, acute HF) → synchronized cardioversion:

Stable:

  • Narrow regular: vagal → adenosine 6 mg → 12 mg → 12 mg rapid IV push + flush.
  • Narrow irregular (AF/flutter): rate control (diltiazem, metoprolol); anticoagulate per CHA₂DS₂-VASc.
  • Wide regular monomorphic VT: amiodarone 150 mg over 10 min (may repeat); or procainamide; or sotalol. Adenosine OK if presumed SVT-with-aberrancy.
  • Wide irregular: assume polymorphic VT or pre-excited AF — avoid AV-nodal blockers; Mg 2 g for torsades; cardiovert.

7Post-ROSC care

  • Airway/Breathing: SpO₂ 94–98% (2025) or PaO₂ 60–105 mmHg (avoid hyperoxia); PaCO₂ 35–45; 10 breaths/min; do not hyperventilate.
  • Hemodynamics: SBP > 90 / MAP > 65 — IVF + norepinephrine 0.1–0.5 mcg/kg/min, epinephrine 0.1–0.5 mcg/kg/min, or dopamine 5–20 mcg/kg/min.
  • 12-lead ECG immediately. STEMI / new LBBB / shock-refractory VT/VF / suspected ACS → emergent cath.
  • Temperature control (2025): 32–37.5 °C × ≥ 36 h if comatose. Shift from routine hypothermia → active fever prevention / controlled normothermia. Acceptable: 32–34 °C → 36–37.5 °C, OR 36–37.5 °C alone. Avoid fever × 72 h.
  • Glucose 140–180; treat seizures (continuous EEG if comatose); neuroprognostication only after ≥ 72 h (longer if TTM).

10Pearls — running the code

  • Compressions over everything. Compression fraction > 60% predicts ROSC.
  • Pulse check ≤ 10 sec; if uncertain, resume CPR.
  • Don't stop CPR for IV access or intubation.
  • EtCO₂ sudden ↑ > 10 mmHg = ROSC (check pulse).
  • EtCO₂ < 10 mmHg after 20 min good CPR with no reversible cause → consider termination.
  • Defib first if witnessed VF/pVT with pads ready (don't delay shock for CPR).
  • Stack chest compressions during charging to minimize peri-shock pause.
  • Hands-off only for shock + pulse/rhythm check; everything else (epi, intubation, IV) continues compressions.
  • "Asystole" — confirm leads attached, gain up, two leads — fine VF mimics.
  • PEA → think volume + reversible cause first; bedside echo at next pulse check.
  • Refractory VF: vector change / double-sequential defib usefulness not established (2025) — recurrent VF after a successful shock is more common than shock failure; consider esmolol, Mg, eCPR.
  • Mechanical CPR devices not routine (2025) — consider only when manual CPR not feasible/safe (e.g., transport).
  • Electrical pacing not useful in established cardiac arrest (2025) — pacing is for symptomatic bradycardia, not pulseless rhythms.
  • IV access preferred over IO (2025); IO acceptable when IV cannot be obtained promptly.
  • Pregnancy ≥ 20 wk: left uterine displacement; perimortem C-section by 4 min if no ROSC.
  • Hyperkalemia arrest: Ca gluconate first (membrane stabilization), then insulin/D50, bicarb.
  • TCA OD with wide QRS: NaHCO₃ 1–2 mEq/kg until QRS narrows or pH 7.5.
  • β-blocker / CCB OD: glucagon, calcium, high-dose insulin euglycemia (1 U/kg bolus → 1 U/kg/h).
  • Local-anesthetic toxicity (LAST): intralipid 20% 1.5 mL/kg bolus → 0.25 mL/kg/min.
  • Coronary perfusion pressure > 15 mmHg needed for ROSC (aortic diastolic – RA diastolic).

11Team leadership & closed-loop

  • Designate code leader; leader stands at foot of bed, hands off.
  • Roles: compressor (×2 swap q2 min), airway, monitor/defib, IV/IO + meds, recorder, leader, family liaison.
  • Closed-loop orders: "Epi 1 mg IV — given by Sara at 10:14."
  • Verbalize at each pulse check: rhythm, time of last epi, time of last shock, EtCO₂, H's & T's status.
  • Family presence with a dedicated support person if requested.
  • Debrief — immediate or delayed (formal recommendation, 2025); document times, drugs, shocks, ROSC time.

12When to stop

  • Sustained ROSC.
  • Rhythm asystole > 20 min with high-quality CPR, EtCO₂ < 10 mmHg, no reversible cause.
  • Reversible causes addressed and refractory.
  • Documented advance directive (DNR/POLST) discovered.
  • Provider safety threatened.
  • Special: hypothermia & toxin/PE/pregnancy → CPR longer; eCPR if program available.
