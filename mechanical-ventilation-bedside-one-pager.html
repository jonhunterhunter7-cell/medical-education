2Lung-protective bundle (every patient)

  • VT 4–8 mL/kg PBW · Pplat ≤ 30 · ΔP ≤ 15 · MP ≤ 17 J/min
  • SpO₂ 88–96% (avoid hyperoxia)
  • HOB 30°, oral care, sedation light (RASS 0 to −1) when safe
  • Daily SBT readiness · DVT & stress-ulcer ppx · early mobility
  • Permissive hypercapnia (pH ≥ 7.20) when needed

5ARDS bundle (Berlin / Global 2023)

  • VT 4–6 mL/kg PBW (ARMA)
  • Pplat ≤ 30 · ΔP ≤ 15 (Amato)
  • PEEP/FiO₂ ladder: high-PEEP table for P/F < 200
  • SpO₂ 88–95% · permissive hypercapnia pH ≥ 7.20
  • Prone ≥ 16 h/day if P/F < 150 (PROSEVA)
  • NMB only for refractory dyssynchrony / ΔP > 15 (ROSE neutral)
  • Conservative fluid balance once shock resolved (FACTT)
  • VV-ECMO if P/F < 80 despite optimization (EOLIA)

PEEP ladder (high): FiO₂ .3→.5→.7→.9→1.0 with PEEP 5→14→16→20→24.

6Obstructive (asthma / COPD)

  • Let them exhale — RR 10–14, I:E 1:3–1:5, peak flow 60–80 L/min
  • VT 6 mL/kg PBW; permit hypercapnia (pH ≥ 7.15)
  • PEEP 0–5; match ~80% of auto-PEEP if patient triggering
  • Adjuncts: continuous β₂, IV steroid, IV Mg 2 g, ketamine sedation, heliox
  • Sudden hemodynamic collapse: disconnect from vent → let chest empty → bag with prolonged exhalation; rule out PTX

7Auto-PEEP & asynchrony

Auto-PEEP: flow trace not returning to zero; end-exp hold to quantify. Fix: ↓ RR, ↓ VT, ↑ Te (↑ peak flow / ↓ Ti), bronchodilators, treat cause.

AsynchronyFix
Ineffective trigger (dip without breath)↓ trigger threshold; ↓ auto-PEEP; ↓ sedation
Double trigger (stacked breaths)↑ Ti, ↑ VT, treat anxiety/pain; sedate; NMB if severe ARDS
Reverse triggeringRecognize; deepen sedation or NMB in severe ARDS
Flow starvation (scooped P-trace)↑ peak flow (VC) or PS; switch to decel flow
Cycle (early/late)Adjust expiratory cycle threshold (default 25%; → 10–15% in COPD; → 35–40% in stiff lungs)

8Acute deterioration — DOPES / DOTTS

DOPES: Displaced ETT · Obstruction · Pneumothorax · Equipment · Stacked breaths.

DOTTS (do): Disconnect (bag w/ PEEP valve, FiO₂ 1.0) · Oxygenate · Tube position (capno + auscultation) · Tweak vent · Sonography (PoCUS for PTX, sliding, ETT depth).

  1. PIP↑ Pplat normal → resistance (suction, bronchodilator, kink, bite, cuff over-inflation).
  2. PIP↑ Pplat↑ → compliance (PTX, atelectasis, edema, ↑ IAP).
  3. Sudden ↓SpO₂: tube/PTX/atelectasis/PE/edema/asynchrony.
  4. Sudden ↑PaCO₂: ↑ production (fever, sepsis, shivering, RQ), ↑ Vd (PE, low CO), ↓ VA (auto-PEEP, leak, sedation, weakness).

9Liberation — daily SBT

Readiness: cause improving · FiO₂ ≤ 0.4 · PEEP ≤ 5–8 · stable hemodynamics · adequate cough · awake (RASS −1 to 0) · no active ICP/ischemia.

SBT: PSV 5–8 / PEEP 5 × 30–120 min (or T-piece).

Pass criteria: RR < 35 · VT > 5 mL/kg PBW · RSBI < 105 · NIF < −20 to −25 · SpO₂ ≥ 90% · stable HR/BP · no diaphoresis or accessory-muscle use.

Extubation gates: mental status (follows commands) · adequate cough · manageable secretions · cuff-leak present (≥ 110 mL) — give IV steroid 4 h prior if leak absent and risk factors.

Post-extubation: HFNC default. High-risk (HF, COPD, age > 65, ≥ 7 days vent): HFNC + intermittent BiPAP 10/5 ≥ 4 h/day × 48 h (Thille 2019: ↓ reintubation, ↓ 90-day mortality).

11Top PCCM pearls

  • Track ΔP and Pplat, not just FiO₂/PEEP — they're the mortality-correlated variables.
  • The two-pressure question: PIP and Pplat tell you resistance vs compliance.
  • Auto-PEEP makes triggering hard — match ~80% of total PEEP. Te ≥ 3τ avoids trapping.
  • Permissive hypercapnia is fine; pH 7.20 (ARDS) / 7.15 (asthma) is the floor.
  • Prone for < 150 P/F — early; > 16 h/day.
  • Don't routinely paralyze; do paralyze if ΔP > 15 despite optimization, refractory dyssynchrony, or while establishing prone/ECMO.
  • Conservative oxygenation: SpO₂ 88–96%; hyperoxia is harmful.
  • Fluid responsiveness: PPV > 13% (controlled mode, sinus rhythm, VT ≥ 8 only); otherwise PLR ≥ 10% (works in any rhythm/mode); EEO ≥ 5%.
  • Helmet NIV (HENIVOT) for de novo hypoxemia — PEEP 10–12 with flow ≥ 50 L/min; reduces intubation vs HFNC.
  • Esophageal manometry rescues the chest-wall problem in obesity / pregnancy / abdominal hypertension.
  • P-SILI is real — large spontaneous efforts in early ARDS can worsen lung injury.
  • Bronchoscopy through ETT: 5.7 mm scope in 7.5 ETT fills 64% lumen → switch to PCV; FiO₂ 1.0 × 5 min before, hold elevated FiO₂ × 1 h after.
  • VV-ECMO refer EARLY when P/F < 80 despite optimal management — transport mortality is high otherwise.
  • Tracheostomy timing doesn't change mortality; pick on day 10–14 if > 7 more vent-days expected.
